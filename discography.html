Backstreet Boys (1996)

The self-titled international debut studio album by American boyband Backstreet Boys. The album was released around Europe, Asia, Canada, and in a few other markets. It was a success, beocming on of the biggest debut albums ever and contains a number of the band's most memorable singles.

Backstreet's Back (1997)

Second studio album by the Backstreet Boys released internationally August 11, 1997 by Jive Records and Trans Continental Records, with the exception of the United States. It was a follow up success to their sucessful self-titled international debut.

Millennium (1999)

Third album by the American boy band the Backstreet Boys. It was a highly anticipated follow-up to both their U.S. debut album, and their second internationally released album. It was their first album to be released in both the U.S. and internationally in the same form, at the same time.The self-titled international debut studio album by American boyband Backstreet Boys.

Black & Blue (2000)

Fourth (third in the U.S.) album of the American vocal-pop group Backstreet Boys. It is their follow-up album from their 1999 studio release Millennium. The album recorded the best international sales in a week for an album in history by selling over 5 million copies in its first week of sales globally. In the United States, Black & Blue sold 1.6 million copies in its first week of release, making the Backstreet Boys the first group in Soundscan history to have million-plus first-week sales with back-to-back albums. As of 2007, the album has sold 24 million copies worldwide.

Never Gone (2005)

Fifth (fourth in the U.S.) studio album released by American vocal group Backstreet Boys as the follow up to their fourth (third in the U.S.) studio album Black & Blue after a short hiatus. Never Gone was originally due for release in 2004, but the release date for the album was pushed to June 14, 2005 for unknown reasons. The album varies musically from their previous albums, with a rock sound to the album. Unlike previous albums, the album featured only live instruments. The album is named after a song on it which mourns the loss of Kevin Richardson's father.

Unbreakable (2007)

Sixth studio album (fifth in the United States). It was released on October 24, 2007 in Japan and October 30 in the United States by Jive Records. This is the first Backstreet Boys album after Kevin Richardson's departure in 2006 to pursue other interests. Unlike previous Backstreet Boys albums, this album features no collaborations with longtime producers and friends Max Martin and Kristian Lundin who had worked on the group's previous albums.
